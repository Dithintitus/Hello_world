History on how "hello world" came to be.

The origin of the "Hello, World!" program dates back to the early 1970s. It was first used by Brian Kernighan in 1972 to illustrate features of the B programming language, which was a precursor to C. Later, when Dennis Ritchie and Brian Kernighan co-authored the influential book "The C Programming Language," published in 1978, they used "Hello, World!" as the first example in the book to introduce the basics of C programming. The simplicity and universal nature of "Hello, World!" made it an ideal starting point for learners and instructors. Over time, it became a tradition across programming languages, symbolizing the first step in learning how to write, compile, and execute code. It essentially serves as a rite of passage for many developers.
